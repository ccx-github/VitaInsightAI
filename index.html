ABOUT

Currently, cardiovascular disease has become one of the leading causes of death worldwide. Early intervention is crucial for preventing end-stage symptoms of cardiovascular disease. Existing studies have shown that multiple risk factors can have synergistic effects, significantly increasing an individual's overall risk of developing cardiovascular disease (CVD). However, factors such as invasive examinations, inconvenient and time-consuming medical visits, as well as subtle early symptoms may discourage people from seeking hospital diagnosis, leading to missed opportunities for early intervention. To address this, this study developed an integrated monitoring and early intelligent assessment system for chronic cardiovascular disease based on artificial intelligence and non-invasive hardware devices. The research trained 11 AI prediction models using the UCI Cleveland dataset and evaluated them based on multiple metrics including AUC, recall, accuracy, precision, and F1 score. Ultimately, the CatBoost model, with an AUC score of 0.94, was selected for deployment on the hardware device. By combining the CatBoost model with the SHAP interpretability algorithm, the system not only enables early CVD risk assessment but also provides users with personalized risk explanations. Through the integration of an ESP32 main control module, an AD8232 ECG sensor, and multi-functional sensors, the system achieves real-time collection, comprehensive analysis, and cloud transmission of key physiological indicators. Test results show that the device can achieve 90% accuracy in real experimental scenarios, effectively identifying CVD risks while significantly enhancing device transparency and user trust. This study provides a low-cost, convenient early health assessment tool for preventing high-risk cardiovascular disease, offering significant social application and promotion value for use in households, primary healthcare, and remote areas.
